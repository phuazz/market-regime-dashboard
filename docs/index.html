Recession & Market-Peak Dashboard — three lenses on bear-market risk

Proof of concept Data as of —

Recession risk (is the economy turning?), market-peak froth (is the market priced and positioned like a top?), and price trend (is the market actually rolling over?). A single lens firing is not the signal: the risk-reduction signal is Lens 1 elevated or Lens 2 at or above its alarm level, then confirmed by Lens 3. When the combination confirms, the action is to reduce risk, not liquidate — the signals carry false positives.

Every value is from sourced public data with a primary and secondary source per data point. Every threshold is an independently chosen parameter, confirmed by ZH. Nothing on this page is a third-party proprietary figure.

Overall read
Risk-reduction signal: Lens 1 elevated or Lens 2 at its alarm, confirmed by Lens 3 — reduce risk, not liquidate.

When each lens fires: Lens 1 when any one of its six status indicators reaches elevated (the worst-of rule; valuation is context and excluded). This is a deliberately sensitive bar — each indicator's "elevated" is itself a serious, individually-calibrated recession trigger, and a single lens firing is not the signal, so the slots above show the depth (how many indicators are elevated), not just the binary. Lens 2 fires at the 62.5% froth alarm; Lens 3 when the 50-day crosses below the 150-day with both flat or falling. The risk-reduction signal is Lens 1 or Lens 2, then confirmed by Lens 3 — unconfirmed arms cost nothing by design. Thresholds are independently chosen and confirmed; the Lens 2 alarm and the Lens 1 firing bar are documented in filed calibrations.
