Recession risk (is the economy turning?), market-peak froth (is the market priced and positioned like a top?), and price trend (is the market actually rolling over?). A single lens firing is not an act signal: the act rule is Lens 1 elevated or Lens 2 at or above its alarm level, then confirmed by Lens 3. When the combination confirms, the action is to reduce risk, not liquidate — the signals carry false positives.

Every value is from sourced public data with a primary and secondary source per data point. Every threshold is an independently chosen proposed default, pending confirmation. Nothing on this page is a third-party proprietary figure.
